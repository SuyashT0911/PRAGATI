Our Story

How Pragati Came to Life

It started when three college students realized their friends were making career decisions based on what their parents did, or what their neighbor's kid studied — not based on what was actually available to them.

They built a simple spreadsheet of paths. Then it grew. Now it's Pragati.

The Idea — 2023
The Problem Discovered
Three MCA students realized millions of peers were making uninformed education choices. A simple spreadsheet of paths became a mission.
First Build — 2024
Version 1 Launched
The first version of Pragati launched with 50 paths. 1,000 students used it in the first month. The feedback was overwhelming.
Growth — 2025
50,000 Students Guided
Paths expanded to 200+. Certified courses launched. The dashboard, admin panel, and path recommender were built and refined.
Now — 2026
Building for Every Student
Pragati 2.0 — full platform with interactive path explorer, AI-powered recommendations, certified courses, and a community of 50,000+ learners.

FAQ

Common Questions Answered

Is Pragati completely free?
Yes! Path exploration and information are always free. You need an account only to enroll in courses, save paths, and track progress. Account creation is also free.
Which education levels does Pragati cover?
Pragati covers everything — from Class 10th stream selection to PhD programs. Science, Commerce, Arts, Diploma, Graduation, Post-Graduation, and Research paths are all included.
Are the certificates recognized by employers?
Our certificates are issued on completion of courses with verified quiz assessments. They can be added to LinkedIn and shared as PDF. Industry recognition grows as our partner network expands.
How accurate is the salary information?
Salary ranges are based on current NAUKRI, LinkedIn Salary Insights, and placement reports from top institutions. We update them quarterly to reflect market changes.
Can I use Pragati if I'm in Class 10th?
Absolutely! Class 10th stream selection is one of the most important decisions. Pragati helps you understand what each stream leads to before you commit, so you can choose wisely.
